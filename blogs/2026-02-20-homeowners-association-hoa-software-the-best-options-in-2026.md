<h2 class="wp-block-heading alt">What is Homeowner Association Software?</h2>



<p>Homeowner Association Software, or HOA Software, is an all-in-one management tool comprised of a website builder, online contact database, communications system (email), finance &amp; payment system, event platform, and more.</p>



<p>If you run an HOA, nearly all your administrative work can be eliminated by HOA Software, which can:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Keep your residents happier by giving them easy, secure ways to manage their payments and fees, and to submit maintenance requests online.</li>



<li>Improve member communication through automated invoices, emails, newsletters, and maintenance requests.</li>



<li>Give you an easy-to-build and update <a href="https://www.wildapricot.com/blog/hoa-websites" rel="noopener" target="_blank">HOA website</a> where you can host a news section, notices, resources, member directory, an event calendar and more.</li>



<li>Maintain a full contact database that’s easy to search, filter, and update.</li>



<li>Provide you with simple financial reports and analytics.</li>



<li>Save money and time by automating many more administrative tasks.</li>



<li>Allow you to manage your HOA on the go through an HOA app, a mobile version of the software. Members can also use an HOA app to check the member directory and connect with other members.</li>
</ul>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What Features You Need to Look For in HOA Software</h2>



<p>HOA software solutions vary greatly in the features they offer, and it can get overwhelming trying to decide how to spend your software budget wisely. While there are many special features to consider, here are the basics every great HOA software should have.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. Member Database:</strong></h3>



<p>You should be able to maintain a <a href="https://www.wildapricot.com/features/membership-management-software">database of your members</a>, their contact information, member profiles, roles, and preferences. It should also be easy to update their information, contact them, search through the database through different criteria, and track membership status and payments.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. Member Portal:</strong></h3>



<p>Members should be able to log in via a member portal to update their information or access exclusive member-only content. This is also where you would communicate with members or allow them to communicate with each other through chats and forums.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>3. Online Payment System:</strong></h3>



<p>With <span style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">comprehensive HOA software, your members should be able to easily and safely make <a href="https://www.wildapricot.com/features/online-payment-processing" target="_blank">payments online</a>, in pers</span>on, or over the phone. Allowing residents the option of self-service financial tools empowers them to automate and manage their dues and other payments, like late fees and community violations. Your HOA software should also track who has paid their dues and who is overdue, making it easier for you to send accurate invoices.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>4. Communications System:</strong></h3>



<p>Your HOA software should make it easy to <a href="https://www.wildapricot.com/features/contact-database-software">communicate with all your members</a> at once, contact individual members, or create lists filtered by specific criteria. After all, a great community association software supports various modes of communication — emails, letters, push notifications, etc — and keeps track of members’ communications preferences.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>5. <a href="https://www.wildapricot.com/features/online-event-registration-software">Event Management System</a>:&nbsp;</strong></h3>



<p>You should be able to schedule events, maintain an online event calendar and manage attendees. Your members should be able to register for events, pay event fees and update their RSVP status easily in your HOA software.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>6. Financial Tracking and Reporting:</strong></h3>



<p>Your HOA software should maintain accurate financial records and allow you to pull reports and analyze data at any time. Handling HOA accounting can get complex, especially with larger communities. Being able to pull up financial statements and filter by fields like homeowner, date and payment type is crucial for simplifying operations.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>7. Manage Homeowner Requests:</strong></h3>



<p>In any community with property management, homeowners will have requests. HOA software should be able to accept these maintenance requests and enable the community manager to solve any issues in a timely manner. In their owner portals, residents need the ability to send their requests for things like general maintenance, emergency issues, landscaping, community violations and even neighborhood suggestions. Commonly, this will be done via online forms rather than communication tools like email to simplify and ensure the request is routed appropriately.</p>
