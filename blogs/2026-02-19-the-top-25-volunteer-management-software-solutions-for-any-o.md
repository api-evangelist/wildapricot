<h2 class="wp-block-heading">How to Use WildApricot for Volunteer Management</h2>



<p>As we mentioned before, WildApricot is an all-in-one nonprofit management option used by over 20,000 organizations (<a href="https://register.wildapricot.com/OpenFreeAccountNew.aspx">here’s a link to the free, 60-day trial</a>). Though it’s not a tailored solution for volunteer management like others in this list, if you’re already using WildApricot to manage your members, or are looking for an inexpensive solution that allows you to combine member and volunteer management, it’s a worthy option to consider.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Below, we’ve listed three easy ways you can use it to combine member management and volunteer management all in one place.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">1. Ask Your Members for Their Availability</h3>



<p>If it’s a simple need of members letting their availability be known, you can create a specific section in membership fields called something like Volunteer Opportunity. The example we have shows Interests below, but you can easily change this to Volunteer Opportunity, allowing your volunteers to select the opportunities they&#8217;d like to sign up for.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img alt="" class="wp-image-13518" height="279" src="https://www.wildapricot.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/adding-list-items-WA.png" width="370" /></figure>



<p>Creating a multiple-choice field in this section listing all the opportunities your organization offers allows volunteers to log in and simply let their preferences be known. The admin can then run an advanced search on that field and the values to find out who is available. This is a simple workaround for a simple need.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">2. Embed Your Favourite Volunteer Scheduling Software</h3>



<p>WildApricot allows you to embed any platform that provides an embed code for their platform. You can simply create a page for volunteers to schedule themselves and embed the code in that page.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">3. Integrate With Google Calendar Slots</h3>



<p>It is possible to <a href="https://it.stonybrook.edu/help/kb/creating-bookable-appointment-slots-in-google-calendar">create appointment booking slots in Google Calendar</a>. You can then embed this calendar on any page. Then, <a href="https://www.wildapricot.com/blogs/newsblog/2019/01/11/wild-apricot-integromat"><u>using our integrations</u></a>, you can record the event on a specific member’s profile.</p>



<p>In this solution, the volunteer would book a slot on the embedded Google Calendar, and Make (previously Integromat) would then look up that member and make a note on their WildApricot profile. This would make it possible to run advanced searches and get a report on who volunteered on a specific date.</p>
